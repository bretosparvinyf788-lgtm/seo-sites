Evidence and update standards

Research Methodology

Our methodology separates product discovery from proof. A working listing, a seller image, an old buyer photo and a warehouse image of the exact ordered variant are different levels of evidence.

Link review

We check whether a source URL loads, whether the product still matches the stated category, whether the intended variants remain available and whether price or domestic freight has materially changed. A working link does not guarantee product quality.

QC evidence grading

  • Exact order evidence: warehouse photos of the user’s own item.
  • Exact listing and variant: prior warehouse photos connected to the same source and option.
  • Same seller or model: useful context, but weaker than an exact variant match.
  • Visually similar example: discovery evidence only and should not be presented as proof.

Measurements and shipping factors

We prioritize measurements over marketplace letter sizes. Weight, packaging and dimensions are included when they can materially affect landed cost. Volumetric weight, route restrictions and current carrier terms must still be verified with the active service.

Updates and corrections

Pages are updated when a link dies, a route changes, a statement becomes materially inaccurate or new evidence changes the decision framework. Historical QC should not be described as current proof. Reported corrections are reviewed against evidence before publication.

What the methodology cannot guarantee

No methodology can guarantee stock, seller behavior, future production quality, agent performance, customs outcomes or delivery. The purpose is to make uncertainty visible and improve the quality of the user’s own checks.
