Heavyweight hoodies

Record actual weight because thick fabric can make a low product price expensive after shipping.

Measurements and construction checks

  • Compare chest width, total length and sleeve length with a reference hoodie.
  • Check the hood from front, side and rear angles for proportion and seam alignment.
  • Inspect embroidery density, print placement and cracking or uneven edges.
  • Review cuffs, waistband, pockets, drawstrings and zipper hardware.
  • Confirm packaged weight before combining several heavyweight pieces.
Evidence rule: an old QC set can show common characteristics, but the photos and measurements of your own warehouse item remain the strongest evidence.

Use hoodies efficiently in a haul

Hoodies often take more parcel weight than T-shirts and small accessories. Build around one or two well-verified pieces, compare their actual warehouse weights and remove only packaging that does not protect the item. Do not assume that a low item price means a low landed cost.

Hoodie Spreadsheet FAQ

What does a useful hoodie entry include?

A live source link, current variant, measurements, weight, seller context, QC evidence and last-check date.

Is GSM always reliable?

No. Seller GSM claims may be inconsistent. Actual item weight, fabric appearance and construction provide stronger context.

How do I check oversized sizing?

Compare measured chest and length with a hoodie you own rather than relying only on the word oversized.

What extra QC photo is useful?

A measurement photo, direct rear view and close-up of the print or embroidery can resolve common uncertainty.

Why are hoodies expensive to ship?

Dense fabric and bulky folds increase actual weight and sometimes parcel volume.
