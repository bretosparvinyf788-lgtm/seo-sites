Small add-ons

Confirm scale, material, closures, moving parts and route restrictions before treating an item as easy parcel filler.

What to verify

  • Record actual dimensions instead of relying on product photos for scale.
  • Inspect clasps, buckles, adjustment hardware and other moving parts.
  • Check material descriptions and whether the route has restrictions.
  • Use close-up QC photos for engraving, embroidery and surface finish.
  • Compare the value of the item with domestic freight and parcel complexity.
Small does not always mean simple: batteries, liquids, magnets or restricted materials can affect route eligibility even when the item is light.

Use accessories deliberately

Accessories can improve parcel efficiency when they are lightweight, verified and compatible with the route. Avoid adding uncertain filler products simply because the product price is low. A small item with unclear sizing, weak hardware or a restricted material can create more work than value.

Accessories Spreadsheet FAQ

What should an accessory entry include?

A working source link, dimensions, material, current price, hardware notes, weight and useful QC evidence.

How do I check belt sizing?

Compare the usable hole range and total length with a belt that fits, not only the labeled size.

Why do materials matter?

Material affects durability, appearance, cleaning and sometimes shipping-line eligibility.

Are accessories good parcel fillers?

Only when they are useful, verified and route-compatible. Low price alone is not enough.
