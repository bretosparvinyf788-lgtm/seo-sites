Bag QC priorities

  • Measure width, height, depth and strap length.
  • Inspect zippers, clasps, rings, buckles and attachment points.
  • Check symmetry, shape, edge finish and stitching.
  • Request interior, base and side photos when details are missing.
  • Compare actual weight with packaging dimensions.
Shipping rule: bags can be light but bulky. Protective boxes and inserts may increase volumetric weight.

Balance protection and parcel efficiency

Rigid packaging may protect shape but increase parcel volume. Soft bags may ship more efficiently but still need protection around hardware and structured panels. Use the warehouse condition and route rules instead of applying one packaging choice to every bag.

Bag Spreadsheet FAQ

What should a bag entry include?

A live source link, current price, dimensions, material notes, weight and useful QC evidence.

What extra QC photo is useful?

Interior, base, side views and hardware close-ups usually answer important questions.

Should I remove a box?

It depends on structure, material, route rules and how much volume the packaging adds.

Why can a light bag cost more to ship?

Large dimensions can create volumetric weight even when actual weight is low.
