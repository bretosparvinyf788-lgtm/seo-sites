CSSBuy Spreadsheet Categories for Smarter Product Research

Organize CSSBuy finds by product type so every link can be compared with the right QC checklist, measurements, weight assumptions and shipping risks.

Use category structure to reduce bad decisions

A long spreadsheet becomes difficult to use when shoes, hoodies, bags and unrelated accessories are mixed together. Category grouping creates smaller comparison sets and lets every product type use the correct quality-control criteria. It also helps estimate parcel composition before items enter the warehouse.

Core CSSBuy spreadsheet categories

Shoes

Compare sizing, shape, construction, box volume and footwear-specific QC evidence.

Clothing & Hoodies

Prioritize chest, length, sleeve measurements, fabric weight, stitching, prints and expected fit.

Bags

Review dimensions, structure, hardware, lining, handles, closures and protective packaging.

Pants & Trousers

Record waist, rise, inseam, thigh width and fabric behavior instead of choosing from a label alone.

Accessories

Check dimensions, materials, finish, functional parts and whether small products have route restrictions.

Electronics

Confirm model, battery rules, included parts, functionality evidence and eligible shipping lines.

Keep category pages current

Show when links were reviewed, flag dead or replaced listings and separate seller claims from observed QC evidence. A product may change while the URL stays the same. Use the main CSSBuy spreadsheet workflow for product research and the CSSBuy QC Finder for evidence matching.

Frequently asked questions

Which categories should a CSSBuy spreadsheet include?

Shoes, clothing, hoodies, bags, accessories, electronics and other groups can be separated according to their QC and shipping needs.

Why split products into category pages?

It improves comparison, applies more relevant QC checks and makes parcel planning easier.

How often should links be checked?

Popular links should be reviewed regularly and changed listings should be flagged quickly.

Are bags inspected like clothing?

No. Bags focus on structure, hardware, lining and dimensions; clothing focuses more on measurements, material and fit.

What is a good first-haul category?

Simple clothing and small accessories are often easier than bulky or restricted products, although destination rules still matter.

How should prices be compared?

Use expected landed cost, including weight, packaging, possible returns and international shipping.
