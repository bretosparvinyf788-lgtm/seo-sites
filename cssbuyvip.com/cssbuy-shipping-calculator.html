Estimate the parcel, not just product weight

Add the expected weight of every item, then include boxes, protective material and the outer carton. Bulky items require dimension estimates because some lines bill by volumetric weight.

Planning principle: calculate a range rather than one exact number. Use a lower scenario for reduced packaging and a higher scenario for full packaging or dimensional billing.

Three planning cases

Actual-weight case

Add product and packaging weight, then apply the route's first-weight and additional-weight rules.

Volumetric case

Multiply length, width and height and divide by the route's published divisor.

Total-cost case

Add route price, optional services, insurance, handling and destination-side charges that may apply.

Frequently asked questions

What does a CSSBuy shipping calculator estimate?

It estimates chargeable weight and supports parcel-cost planning; it does not guarantee a final carrier quote.

What is chargeable weight?

It is the billing weight, often the greater of actual and volumetric weight.

Why can the final quote differ?

Packed dimensions, measured weight, route availability and restrictions can differ from assumptions.

Should I remove shoe boxes?

Removing boxes can reduce weight and volume, but it also reduces protection and collectible packaging.

When should I split a parcel?

Consider splitting when items require different routes or a smaller package gives a better risk-cost balance.

Does the cheapest route always make sense?

No. Compare tracking, delivery time, restrictions, compensation and dimensional billing.
