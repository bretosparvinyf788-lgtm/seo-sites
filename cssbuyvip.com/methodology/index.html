Editorial standards

Editorial and Research Methodology

Our guide methodology is designed for facts that change quickly: source links, warehouse evidence, route rules, packaging practices, coupons and service charges.

QC guidance

We distinguish the user’s own warehouse photos from prior images of the same listing, similar products and seller marketing photos. Exact evidence supports an approval decision; weaker evidence supports only research and comparison.

Shipping information

Shipping pages explain concepts such as actual weight, volumetric weight, packaging and route restrictions. Calculator outputs are estimates, not carrier quotations. Current prices, divisors, limits and destination rules must be confirmed with the active service.

Coupons and fees

A coupon should not be presented as the full cost of a transaction. Guides should distinguish product price, domestic freight, service charges, currency conversion, packaging, international shipping and destination costs. Promotional terms require a date because eligibility can change.

Source and update standards

  • Prefer official current terms for fees, route rules and service policies.
  • Use exact dates for time-sensitive information.
  • Identify estimates and assumptions clearly.
  • Link related guides so users can verify the full decision process.
  • Correct material errors when reliable evidence supports the correction.

Limitations

No editorial process can guarantee a seller, item, agent, customs or delivery outcome. The purpose is to organize checks, explain tradeoffs and prevent unsupported certainty.
